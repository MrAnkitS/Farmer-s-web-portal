Agritech companies seek Govt impetus in budget to power agriculture sector forward

Indian agriculture has been plagued by supply chain problems, lack of modern storage, over dependence on monsoon, and various others. As a result, farmers have been losing their produce. Industries related to the agriculture sector are progressing at quadruple speed day by day, but the income of farmers has not doubled in the last seven years. This is in spite of the fact that doubling farmers’ income is included in the government's major agenda. But in the last few years, agritech startups have done a lot of work in this direction, due to which they have brought revolutionary changes in the agriculture sector.

Agritech startups help in increasing productivity and have contributed immensely in improving the condition of farmers. Notably, the interest of investors in the agritech sector has increased significantly over the years. India is now the third-largest country in terms of receiving funding for agritech startups.

Although there has been significant growth in these startups, they still have a lot of challenges to deal with. Some of these challenges are as follows:

  • Lack of digital awareness in rural areas


  • Very limited access to smartphones among the economically weaker section (marginal landholders)


  • Youth not taking much interest in agriculture and moving to other industries


  • Limited regulations, awareness and infrastructure to support organic food production


  • Scattered data and information due to negligible collaboration between startups and the government
